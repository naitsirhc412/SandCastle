Tutorial

Learn how to get the most out of SandCastle

What is SandCastle?

SandCastle is a financial dashboard for independent restaurants. You upload your bank statement and your sales report — SandCastle does the math and shows you exactly where your money is going, how healthy your business is, and what to focus on next.

No accounting knowledge needed. All data stays on your device — nothing is sent to a server.

1

Upload Your Bank Statement

Click Upload Data in the left menu. The first card is your bank statement. This shows SandCastle your actual cash moving in and out — it's used for the Cash Flow chart and to cross-check your sales numbers.

Export a statement from your bank's website, then drag the file onto the card or click to browse. Accepted formats: CSV, XLS, XLSX, PDF.

💡Most banks let you download a statement as CSV or Excel from your online account under "Statements" or "Transaction History." If you only have a PDF, SandCastle can read that too.
2

Upload Your Sales Report

The second card is your POS (Point of Sale) or sales report. This is what SandCastle uses to calculate revenue, food cost, labor, and all your key metrics. Export this from your POS system (Toast, Square, Lightspeed, etc.).

The report should include a breakdown by category — food, beverages, alcohol, etc. — plus labor and cost of goods if your system tracks it.

💡In Toast: go to Reports → Export. In Square: go to Sales Summary → Export CSV. For other systems, look for "Export" or "Download" on any sales summary or P&L report.
3

Label Your Period & Save

After uploading, scroll down to the Period Label field. Give this upload a name you'll recognize — like "Jan 2024" or "Q1 2024". Then set how many months of data this file covers.

When you're ready, click Save Period. SandCastle will process the data and load it into your dashboard.

💡You can upload multiple periods over time — each one gets its own label. This lets you track trends across months or quarters. Upload all your historical data to build a fuller picture.
4

Reading Your Dashboard

The Dashboard is your home base. The top row shows your key numbers at a glance. Each card is color-coded: green means healthy, amber means watch it, red means action needed.

Use the dropdowns at the top right to filter by time period — view a single month, a quarter, or all your data combined.

💡Click the ? button on any chart or metric card for a plain-English explanation of what it measures and how it's calculated.
5

Using the Next Steps Tab

The Next Steps tab cuts through the noise and tells you the single most important thing to work on. It always looks at all your data combined so nothing gets hidden in one period.

Issues are ranked by severity. Each card explains the problem, gives you Quick Wins (things you can do this week) and Fundamental Fixes (bigger structural changes). Even when everything looks healthy, it will find the metric closest to its limit and suggest ways to improve it.

💡The benchmarks used are sourced from the National Restaurant Association for small independent US full-service restaurants. They're guidelines, not hard rules — your specific concept may have different targets.
6

Using the Forecasting Tab

The Forecasting tab lets you ask "what if?" questions without any risk. Drag the sliders or type in numbers to instantly see how a change would affect your profit.

For example: "What happens to my profit if I raise prices by 5%?" or "How much would I save if I cut payroll by $2,000?" The results update in real time.

💡The Forecasting tab is great for planning ahead — use it before making decisions like adding a staff member, changing your menu prices, or negotiating a new supplier contract.
